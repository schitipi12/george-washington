The Man Who Built a Country

Before the United States existed, one brave leader helped make it possible. George Washington led soldiers through impossible odds, became the first President, and showed the world what a free nation could be.

Early Life

Mount Vernon — Washington's beloved home in Virginia

🏡 Born in Virginia

George Washington was born on February 22, 1732, in Westmoreland County, Virginia. He grew up on a farm along the Potomac River.

📐 School & Learning

George didn't go to college — he mostly learned at home and from tutors. He loved math and maps, and became a skilled land surveyor by age 17!

🌿 Mount Vernon

He eventually owned a large farm called Mount Vernon in Virginia. He grew crops like wheat and corn and cared deeply about the land.

⚔️ Young Soldier

At just 21 years old, Washington became a military officer. He gained early experience fighting in the French and Indian War.

⚔️

The Revolutionary War

When the American colonies decided to fight for independence from Britain, they needed a strong leader. They chose George Washington.

Washington Crossing the Delaware — a famous painting of the Christmas 1776 surprise attack

🎖️ Commander-in-Chief

In 1775, Congress chose Washington to lead the Continental Army — the new American military force fighting for freedom.

🛶 Crossing the Delaware

On a freezing Christmas night in 1776, Washington secretly led his soldiers across the icy Delaware River for a surprise attack — and won!

❄️ Valley Forge

During the brutal winter of 1777–1778, Washington held his starving, freezing army together at Valley Forge through sheer courage.

🎉 Victory!

In 1781, Washington's army defeated the British at Yorktown. The war was over — America had won its independence!

🏛️

First President of the United States

After the war, the new nation needed a leader. Every single elector voted for George Washington — he was the unanimous choice.

Washington presiding over the Constitutional Convention, 1787

🎊 Inauguration Day

On April 30, 1789, Washington was sworn in as the 1st President in New York City. Crowds cheered and bells rang!

📜 Setting the Rules

Everything Washington did as president set an example for all future presidents. He created the Cabinet and established many traditions still followed today.

🔁 Two Terms

Washington served two four-year terms. He chose not to run for a third — showing leaders should peacefully give up power.

🏡 Going Home

In 1797, he retired to Mount Vernon. His famous Farewell Address is still read in the U.S. Senate every year.

Washington's Greatest Achievements

Here are the things that make George Washington truly unforgettable.

🗽

Won American Independence

Led the Continental Army to defeat the most powerful military in the world — the British Empire — securing freedom for the 13 colonies.

📜

Helped Write the Constitution

Presided over the Constitutional Convention of 1787, which created the framework for U.S. government still used today.

🤝

Set the Peaceful Transfer of Power

By voluntarily stepping down after two terms, Washington showed the world that democracy — not kings — could work. This tradition continues today.

🏦

Built a Stable New Nation

As the first president, Washington created the Cabinet, established a national bank, and set up many traditions that still define the U.S. presidency.

⚖️

Warned Against Division

In his Farewell Address, Washington warned Americans not to split into rival political parties or favor foreign nations — wisdom still relevant today.
